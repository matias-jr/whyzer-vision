01 — The experiment

The loser never knew they were losing.

Anthropic ran "Project Deal" — 69 employees, Claude AI agents negotiating on both sides, 186 deals closed with zero human intervention. Then they secretly ran two versions: one where everyone had the frontier model (Opus), one where some got a weaker model (Haiku) without knowing it.

Opus fetched $65 for a ruby. Haiku got $35. Same bicycle: $65 vs $38. Satisfaction scores were identical. The losing side had no idea they were losing.

"Now scale that to your $500K deal. Procurement teams are already using AI agents to score, rank, and filter vendors — before a committee ever convenes."

02 — What sellers need to expect

Four things that are already changing.

The experiment used small purchases. The mechanism is not small. Here's how this plays out at enterprise scale — and what to do about each.

Shortlists form before you call

Procurement AI scores vendors before a human opens your proposal. The RFP in your inbox may already have a preferred winner.

The surprise loss gets more common

Your champion says everything is great. You lose. An AI ranked your business case third on financial rigor. Nobody told your champion.

Your first number is your anchor

AI agents don't get charmed over three rounds. Opening value framing sets the outcome. Your champion can't recover a weak first impression.

Your digital footprint is reviewed

Agents pull your G2 reviews, case studies, LinkedIn. The buyer's AI knows more about your company than most sellers know about their own product.

03 — The checklist

20 things to do before you hit send.

Four stages. Use it the day before every major submission.

Progress0 of 20 complete
Before you write0 / 4
Ask your champion if they use AI-assisted vendor scoring
"What does your evaluation process look like at this stage — is there a formal scoring system your team uses?"
Map every stakeholder whose concerns an AI will flag
CFO = ROI and cost certainty. Legal = data and compliance. IT = security and integration. Procurement = price benchmarking.
Define your specific business outcome — with a number
"Improve efficiency" fails. "Reduce contract review from 14 to 4 days, recovering $240K in annual deal velocity" passes.
Know how your top competitor is positioning in this account
An AI scoring system will compare you. Compare yourself first — then pre-empt the gaps.
Building the proposal0 / 8
Every claimed benefit has a number attached
Time saved, revenue protected, cost avoided, risk reduced — all quantified.
Every number has a source or methodology
"Based on average across 12 similar deployments" beats a naked statistic every time.
Cost of inaction is quantified — not just the upside
AI scoring looks for risk framing. The cost of doing nothing needs a number too.
Implementation risk is addressed directly
Timeline, resources, dependencies, mitigation. Don't make the AI hunt for what it will flag anyway.
Data handling, security, and compliance are addressed
If you sell anything that touches sensitive data or autonomous decisions — answer before they ask.
Differentiation is stated in outcome terms, not feature terms
"The only platform that does X" means nothing. "Clients in your industry reduced Y by Z% within 90 days" passes.
At least one specific reference to their industry, goals, or public commitments
This signals the proposal was built for them — not templated. AI flags generic proposals.
Top two competitor alternatives are named and pre-empted
An AI evaluator will surface them anyway. Better to control that comparison than let the system do it.
Pressure test before sending0 / 5
Run the procurement AI simulation
Paste your full proposal into Claude or GPT-4 with this prompt:

"You are an AI procurement assistant evaluating this vendor proposal for a [purchase type, company size]. Score it on: financial rigor, implementation risk mitigation, competitive differentiation, compliance posture, and specificity. List the three weakest sections and what is missing from each."

Run the CFO test
Read only your executive summary and ROI section. If a CFO reading only these two would not approve budget — rewrite until they would.
Run the legal test
Find every place data, AI, compliance, or security should be addressed — and isn't. Fill every gap with your answer already in place.
Run the blank page test
Ask a colleague: "What do we do, who for, what's the outcome, what's the number?" If they can't answer in 30 seconds — rewrite.
Run the competitor comparison test
Ask the AI: "Comparing this to a typical [competitor] proposal — what are this vendor's three weakest points?" Fix what it finds.

"Comparing this proposal to a typical [competitor name] proposal — what are this vendor's three weakest points relative to a standard competitor in this space?"

After you send0 / 3
Brief your champion on the three strongest points — verbally
Don't assume they read it. They need to advocate in rooms you're not in.
Ask your champion about unstated objections
"Is there anyone in legal, finance, or IT who might raise a concern we haven't addressed? I'd rather solve it now."
If you lose — ask for the scoring breakdown
Most teams won't share it. Some will. The ones who do tell you exactly what the AI flagged. That intel is worth more than the deal you just lost.
